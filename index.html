The Synapse - Neurons Don't Touch!

üìÇ INSERT: Page 18 synapse structure diagram from Neurones_and_synapses.pdf

The Problem:

Neurons have a GAP between them (synaptic cleft) - electrical signal can't jump across!

The Solution:

Use CHEMICAL messengers (neurotransmitters) to carry message across gap

Three Parts of a Synapse:

  • Presynaptic neuron = Sending neuron (has vesicles with neurotransmitters)
  • Synaptic cleft = Gap (20-40 nanometers wide)
  • Postsynaptic neuron = Receiving neuron (has receptors)

How Synapses Work - 7 Steps

üìÇ INSERT: Page 22 numbered synapse transmission diagram from Neurones_and_synapses.pdf
  1. ‚ö° Action potential arrives at axon terminal
  2. üö™ Calcium channels OPEN ‚Üí Ca¬≤‚Å∫ enters cell
  3. üì¶ Calcium triggers vesicles to move toward membrane
  4. üîó Vesicles FUSE with membrane (exocytosis)
  5. üíä Neurotransmitters RELEASED into synaptic cleft
  6. üîë Neurotransmitters BIND to receptors on next neuron
  7. ‚ö° New action potential starts in postsynaptic neuron

Neuromuscular Junction

üìÇ INSERT: Page 24 neuromuscular junction diagram from Neurones_and_synapses.pdf

Special Synapse Between Nerve and Muscle

  • Neurotransmitter used: Acetylcholine (ACh)
  • What happens:
    1. ACh released from motor neuron
    2. ACh binds to receptors on muscle
    3. Sodium channels open in muscle
    4. Muscle cell depolarizes
    5. Muscle CONTRACTS
  • Clean up: Enzyme (acetylcholinesterase) breaks down ACh so muscle can relax
